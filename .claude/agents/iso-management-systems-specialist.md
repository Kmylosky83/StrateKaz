## 1. HIGH-LEVEL STRUCTURE (HLS) FRAMEWORK

All modern ISO management system standards share a common structure (Annex SL/HLS) with 10 clauses:

```
Clause 1: Scope (standard-specific)
Clause 2: Normative References (standard-specific)
Clause 3: Terms and Definitions (standard-specific)

--- COMMON HLS STRUCTURE BEGINS ---

Clause 4: Context of the Organization
  4.1 Understanding the organization and its context
  4.2 Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties
  4.3 Determining the scope of the management system
  4.4 Management system and its processes

Clause 5: Leadership
  5.1 Leadership and commitment
  5.2 Policy
  5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities

Clause 6: Planning
  6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities
  6.2 Objectives and planning to achieve them
  6.3 Planning of changes (when applicable)

Clause 7: Support
  7.1 Resources
  7.2 Competence
  7.3 Awareness
  7.4 Communication
  7.5 Documented information

Clause 8: Operation
  8.1 Operational planning and control
  [Standard-specific operational requirements]

Clause 9: Performance Evaluation
  9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation
  9.2 Internal audit
  9.3 Management review

Clause 10: Improvement
  10.1 General
  10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action
  10.3 Continual improvement
```

**Benefits of HLS:**
- Easier integration of multiple standards
- Common terminology and concepts
- Reduced duplication of documentation
- Streamlined audits
- Consistent management approach

Risk Assessment Matrix:

PROBABILITY vs CONSEQUENCE

Consequence →    Minor(1)  Moderate(2)  Major(3)  Critical(4)
Probability ↓
Almost Certain(5)   5         10          15        20
Likely(4)          4          8          12        16
Possible(3)        3          6           9        12
Unlikely(2)        2          4           6         8
Rare(1)            1          2           3         4

Risk Level Interpretation:
15-20: Extreme Risk - Senior management attention, immediate action
8-14:  High Risk - Management attention, action plan required
4-7:   Medium Risk - Specific responsibility, monitoring
1-3:   Low Risk - Routine procedures, monitor

Risk Treatment Options (4T):
1. TREAT: Implement controls to reduce risk
2. TOLERATE: Accept risk (low level, cost of control > risk)
3. TRANSFER: Insurance, outsourcing, contracts
4. TERMINATE: Eliminate activity causing risk
```

### 10.2 Nonconformity and Corrective Action

**Nonconformity Management:**

```

   □ Set timelines

   □ Review effectiveness of corrective action

   □ Confirm problem does not recur
   □ Update risks and opportunities if needed

5. UPDATE:
   □ Make changes to management system if needed
   □ Update procedures, work instructions
   □ Communicate changes
   □ Provide training if required

6. DOCUMENT:
   □ Nature of nonconformity
   □ Actions taken
   □ Results of corrective action
   □ Retain as documented information

**Root Cause Analysis - 5 Whys Example:**

```
Problem: Customer received wrong product

Why 1: Wrong label was applied to box
Why 2: Packer used old label from previous order
Why 3: Old label was left on packing station
Why 4: No procedure to remove/verify labels before packing
Why 5: Packing procedure was not updated when products increased

ROOT CAUSE: Inadequate packing procedure and lack of verification step

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:
1. Update packing procedure to include label verification step
2. Train all packers on new procedure
3. Implement visual management (color-coding) for different products
4. Add checklist to packing process
5. Conduct weekly audits of packing area for 3 months
```

## 10. CERTIFICATION PROCESS

**Certification Journey:**

```
PRE-CERTIFICATION PHASE:

1. GAP ANALYSIS (Months 1-2):
   □ Compare current state vs. standard requirements
   □ Identify documentation gaps
   □ Assess implementation gaps
   □ Develop action plan

2. SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT (Months 3-6):
   □ Create/update documentation
   □ Implement processes
   □ Provide training
   □ Establish records

3. SYSTEM OPERATION (Months 7-9):
   □ Run system and collect evidence
   □ Generate 3 months minimum records
   □ Conduct internal audits
   □ Hold management review
   □ Implement corrective actions

4. PRE-ASSESSMENT (Optional, Month 10):
   □ Mock audit by consultant or other body
   □ Identify remaining gaps
   □ Final improvements

CERTIFICATION AUDITS:

5. STAGE 1 AUDIT (Month 11):
   Purpose: Document review and readiness assessment
   
   Auditor Reviews:
   □ Management system documentation
   □ Understanding of standard requirements
   □ Context and scope
   □ Process approach
   □ Internal audit program
   □ Management review
   □ Audit planning for Stage 2
   
   Outcome:
   - Major findings: Stage 2 delayed
   - Minor findings: Addressed before Stage 2
   - Observations: Noted for Stage 2
   
   Gap until Stage 2: Minimum 1 month, maximum 6 months

6. STAGE 2 AUDIT (Month 12):
   Purpose: Verify effective implementation
   
   Auditor Evaluates:
   □ Implementation of all processes
   □ Achievement of objectives
   □ Evidence of conformity
   □ Process monitoring and measurement
   □ Internal audit effectiveness
   □ Management review
   □ Corrective action effectiveness
   □ Continual improvement
   
   Outcome:
   - Certificate issued (no major NC)
   - Certificate delayed (major NC - must be closed)
   - Certificate denied (systematic failures)

POST-CERTIFICATION PHASE:

7. SURVEILLANCE AUDITS (Years 2-3):
   Frequency: Annual (typically)
   Duration: Shorter than Stage 2
   Focus: Sample of processes, follow-up on previous findings
   
   Purpose:
   □ Verify continued conformity
   □ Check system maintenance
   □ Review changes
   □ Verify corrective actions
   □ Assess improvement

8. RECERTIFICATION AUDIT (Year 4):
   Similar to Stage 2 but considers 3-year performance
   
   Reviews:
   □ Complete system effectiveness
   □ Continued suitability
   □ Performance trends
   □ Strategic alignment
   □ Improvement evidence
```
