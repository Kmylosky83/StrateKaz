## 1. ISO 31000:2018 FRAMEWORK

### Core Principles (8 Principles)

ISO 31000 is built on 8 principles that should characterize effective risk management:

```
1. INTEGRATED
Risk management is an integral part of all organizational activities
- Embedded in all processes and decision-making
- Not a standalone activity
- Part of management responsibilities at all levels

2. STRUCTURED AND COMPREHENSIVE
A structured and comprehensive approach contributes to consistent and comparable results
- Systematic methodology
- Covers all risks across organization
- Applies consistently across different areas

3. CUSTOMIZED
Risk management framework and process are customized and proportionate to:
- Organization's external and internal context
- Risk profile
- Organizational objectives

4. INCLUSIVE
Appropriate and timely involvement of stakeholders enables:
- Their knowledge and views to be considered
- Better awareness and informed risk management
- Representation of different perspectives

5. DYNAMIC
Risks can emerge, change or disappear as contexts change
- Anticipates, detects, acknowledges changes
- Responds appropriately and in timely manner
- Continuous monitoring and review

6. BEST AVAILABLE INFORMATION
Inputs to risk management are based on:
- Historical and current information
- Future expectations
- Considers limitations and uncertainties
- Uses best available data and expertise

7. HUMAN AND CULTURAL FACTORS
Human behavior and culture significantly influence all aspects of risk management
- Recognizes capabilities, perceptions and intentions
- Considers organizational culture
- Addresses human factors in all processes

8. CONTINUAL IMPROVEMENT
Risk management is continually improved through:
- Learning and experience
- Regular review and adaptation
- Performance monitoring
- Feedback loops and lessons learned
```

### Integrated Risk Analysis Matrix

**Unified Assessment Scale:**

```
LIKELIHOOD/PROBABILITY SCALE (1-5):

5 - ALMOST CERTAIN (>80% probability)
Expected to occur in most circumstances
- Has occurred multiple times recently
- Known systemic issues exist
- Historical data shows high frequency

4 - LIKELY (60-80% probability)
Will probably occur in most circumstances
- Has occurred several times
- Strong possibility based on experience
- Emerging patterns indicate occurrence

3 - POSSIBLE (40-60% probability)
Might occur at some time
- Has occurred occasionally
- Could happen under certain conditions
- Some indicators present

2 - UNLIKELY (20-40% probability)
Could occur at some time
- Has rarely occurred
- Requires unusual circumstances
- Few indicators present

1 - RARE (<20% probability)
May only occur in exceptional circumstances
- Has never occurred or extremely rarely
- Requires multiple unlikely events
- No indicators present

CONSEQUENCE/IMPACT SCALE (1-5):

Consider impacts across multiple dimensions:
- Financial (costs, revenue loss, fines)
- Operational (disruption, delivery failure)
- Reputational (brand, stakeholder confidence)
- People (injuries, health, morale)
- Environmental (pollution, resource impact)
- Compliance (legal, regulatory, standards)
- Strategic (objectives, competitive position)

5 - CATASTROPHIC
- Fatalities or permanent disabilities
- Business closure or bankruptcy
- Massive environmental damage
- Permanent reputation destruction
- Complete regulatory shutdown
- Strategic objective failure
- >$1M financial impact (adjust to context)

4 - MAJOR
- Serious injuries or long-term health effects
- Significant business disruption (>1 month)
- Major environmental incident
- Severe reputation damage
- Major regulatory penalties
- Critical objective compromise
- $500K-$1M financial impact

3 - MODERATE
- Medical treatment injuries
- Medium business disruption (1-4 weeks)
- Moderate environmental impact
- Reputation damage requiring management
- Regulatory warnings or minor fines
- Important objective delay
- $100K-$500K financial impact

2 - MINOR
- First aid injuries
- Minor disruption (<1 week)
- Minimal environmental impact
- Limited reputation impact
- Regulatory notification required
- Minor objective impact
- $10K-$100K financial impact

1 - NEGLIGIBLE
- No injuries
- Negligible disruption
- No environmental impact
- No reputation impact
- No regulatory issues
- No objective impact
- <$10K financial impact

RISK LEVEL MATRIX:

Consequence →     1         2         3         4         5
Likelihood ↓   Negligible Minor   Moderate  Major  Catastrophic

5-Almost Certain  5-H      10-E     15-E     20-E     25-E
4-Likely          4-M       8-H     12-E     16-E     20-E
3-Possible        3-L       6-M      9-H     12-E     15-E
2-Unlikely        2-L       4-M      6-M      8-H     10-E
1-Rare            1-L       2-L      3-L      4-M      5-H

Risk Level Legend:
E (Extreme): 15-25 - Executive attention, immediate action mandatory
H (High):    8-14  - Senior management attention, action plan required
M (Medium):  4-7   - Management attention, specific monitoring
L (Low):     1-3   - Routine procedures, periodic review

INHERENT vs RESIDUAL RISK:

Inherent Risk: Risk level BEFORE controls
Residual Risk: Risk level AFTER controls implemented

Risk Reduction Calculation:
Risk Reduction = (Inherent Risk - Residual Risk) / Inherent Risk × 100%

Target: Reduce all Extreme risks to High or lower
        Reduce all High risks to Medium or lower (where reasonably practicable)
```

- Unethical conduct or corruption

RISK TOLERANCE LEVELS:

Risk tolerance defines the specific maximum risk levels acceptable:

Category: Safety/Health (ISO 45001/SST/PESV)
Tolerance: ZERO serious injuries or fatalities
Metric: Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR) < 1.0
Action Trigger: Any serious injury triggers immediate investigation

Category: Environmental (ISO 14001)
Tolerance: No environmental incidents with external impact
Metric: Environmental incidents = 0 per year
Action Trigger: Any incident with community impact

Category: Quality (ISO 9001)
Tolerance: Customer complaints <1% of orders
Metric: Complaint rate tracked monthly
Action Trigger: Rate exceeds 1.5% for two consecutive months

Category: Financial
Tolerance: No single risk exposure >5% annual revenue
Metric: Financial risk register reviewed quarterly
Action Trigger: Any risk approaching 3% of revenue

Category: Compliance
Tolerance: 100% compliance with legal requirements
Metric: Compliance audits and inspections
Action Trigger: Any regulatory warning or violation

## 5. RISK TREATMENT STRATEGIES

### The Four T's - Detailed Application

```
1. TREAT (Modify the Risk)
   Apply controls to reduce likelihood or consequence
   
   Hierarchy of Controls (from most to least effective):
   
   a) ELIMINATION:
      - Remove the hazard/risk entirely
      - Discontinue high-risk activities
      - Redesign to eliminate risk
      Example: Eliminate manual lifting by automating
   
   b) SUBSTITUTION:
      - Replace with less risky alternative
      - Change process/material/equipment
      Example: Replace toxic chemical with safer alternative
   
   c) ENGINEERING CONTROLS:
      - Physical modifications
      - Guards, barriers, ventilation
      - Fail-safe designs
      Example: Install machine guarding, implement GPS speed limiters
   
   d) ADMINISTRATIVE CONTROLS:
      - Procedures and policies
      - Training and supervision
      - Job rotation, work permits
      Example: Implement fatigue management policy, safety procedures
   
   e) PPE (Personal Protective Equipment):
      - Last line of defense
      - Requires user compliance
      Example: Safety glasses, helmets, seatbelts

2. TOLERATE (Accept the Risk)
   Accept risk when:
   - Risk is within tolerance
   - Cost of treatment exceeds benefit
   - No practical treatment available
   - Risk is very low
   
   Requirements:
   - Documented decision
   - Senior management approval
   - Monitoring plan
   - Regular review
   - Contingency plan if risk materializes

3. TRANSFER (Share the Risk)
   Move risk to third party:
   
   a) INSURANCE:
      - Property insurance
      - Liability insurance
      - Business interruption
      - Workers compensation (ARL)
      - Fleet insurance
   
   b) CONTRACTUAL TRANSFER:
      - Outsourcing
      - Warranties and guarantees
      - Service level agreements
      - Hold harmless clauses
   
   c) PARTNERSHIPS:
      - Joint ventures
      - Strategic alliances
      - Risk sharing agreements
   
   Note: Transfer shifts financial consequence, but responsibility remains

4. TERMINATE (Eliminate Activity)
   Stop the activity causing the risk:
   - Discontinue product/service
   - Exit market/geography
   - Cancel project
   - Sell business unit
   
   When appropriate:
   - Risk exceeds all tolerance levels
   - No effective treatment available
   - Strategic misalignment
   - Reputational concerns

COMBINATION STRATEGIES:
Most risks require multiple treatment strategies:

Example - Chemical Handling Risk:
- Treat: Substitute less hazardous chemicals where possible
- Treat: Install ventilation systems (engineering)
- Treat: Implement handling procedures (administrative)
- Treat: Provide PPE (last resort)
- Transfer: Insurance for spills and injuries
- Tolerate: Residual risk after all controls (documented)
```
