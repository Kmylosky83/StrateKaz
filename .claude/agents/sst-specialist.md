## 1. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

### Decreto 1072/2015 Structure

**Book 2, Part 2, Title 4, Chapter 6: SG-SST**

The SG-SST is defined as "a logical, phased process based on continuous improvement including policy, organization, planning, implementation, evaluation, auditing and improvement actions aimed at anticipating, recognizing, evaluating and controlling risks that may affect workplace safety and health."

**Key Articles:**
- Art. 2.2.4.6.4: Employer's obligation to implement SG-SST
- Art. 2.2.4.6.5: Coverage - All workers regardless of contract type
- Art. 2.2.4.6.8: SG-SST objectives and structure
- Art. 2.2.4.6.12: Required documentation
- Art. 2.2.4.6.16: Health surveillance programs
- Art. 2.2.4.6.24: Emergency preparedness and response

### Resolución 0312/2019 - Minimum Standards

**Three Implementation Levels Based on Company Size and Risk:**

**Level 1: Basic (Small companies)**
- 1-10 workers with Risk Class I, II, or III
- 7 minimum standards to comply
- Simplified documentation requirements
- Technical SST professional with minimum 1 year experience

**Level 2: Standard (Medium companies)**
- 11-50 workers with Risk Class I, II, or III
- 21 minimum standards to comply
- More robust documentation system
- COPASST required
- Annual training program

**Level 3: Advanced (Large companies or high risk)**
- 50+ workers with any Risk Class
- OR any company with 1+ workers in Risk Class IV or V
- 61 minimum standards to comply (100 point scale)
- Complete SG-SST implementation
- Annual audits required
- Psychosocial risk battery mandatory

**Self-Assessment Deadlines:**
- Annual self-assessment in December
- Report through SGRL platform: sgrl.mintrabajo.gov.co
- Improvement plan based on gaps identified
- Next year's annual work plan

**Step 4: Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment (GTC 45)**

**Methodology:**
1. **Preparation**: Define scope, criteria, and team
2. **Information Gathering**: Processes, tasks, materials, equipment
3. **Hazard Identification**: By process and activity
4. **Risk Assessment**: Probability and consequence
5. **Risk Evaluation**: Acceptance criteria
6. **Control Measures**: Hierarchy of controls
7. **Documentation**: Hazard matrix

**Hazard Categories (GTC 45):**
- Biological: Viruses, bacteria, fungi, parasites
- Physical: Noise, vibration, radiation, temperature, lighting, pressure
- Chemical: Gases, vapors, dust, fumes, liquids, solids
- Psychosocial: Workload, work conditions, interpersonal relations
- Biomechanical: Posture, repetitive movements, manual handling
- Mechanical: Machinery, tools, falling objects, sharp elements
- Electrical: High/low voltage, static electricity
- Locative: Floors, stairs, storage, workspace design
- Natural phenomena: Earthquakes, floods, landslides
- Transit: Vehicle accidents, roadway conditions
- Public order: Robbery, assault, terrorism
- Work at heights: Falls from different levels
- Confined spaces: Oxygen deficiency, toxic atmosphere

**Risk Level Calculation:**
```
Risk Level = Probability Level × Consequence Level

Probability Level (Low=2, Medium=6, High=10)
Consequence Level (Slight=10, Moderate=25, Serious=60, Very Serious=100)

Risk Interpretation:
I (4000-600): Not Acceptable - Immediate intervention required
II (500-150): Not Acceptable or Acceptable with Control - Urgent intervention
III (120-40): Acceptable - Maintain control measures
IV (20-0): Acceptable - No immediate action needed
```

**Control Hierarchy:**
1. **Elimination**: Remove the hazard
2. **Substitution**: Replace with less hazardous alternative
3. **Engineering Controls**: Isolate people from hazard (guards, ventilation)
4. **Administrative Controls**: Change work procedures, training, signage
5. **PPE**: Personal protective equipment (last resort)

```

  * 10-49 workers: 1 principal + 1 alternate per party
```

**Step 15: SST Indicators**

**Structure Indicators (20%):**
Measure SG-SST design and implementation

```
Examples:
- Policy signed and communicated: Yes=100%, No=0%
- Annual work plan exists: Yes=100%, No=0%
- COPASST functioning: Yes=100%, No=0%
- Hazard matrix updated: Yes=100%, No=0%
```

**Process Indicators (60%):**
Measure SG-SST execution and development

```
Training Compliance:
= (Workers trained / Total workers) × 100

Inspection Compliance:
= (Inspections conducted / Inspections planned) × 100

Corrective Action Closure:
= (Actions closed / Total actions) × 100

Training Hours per Worker:
= Total training hours / Total workers
```

**Result Indicators (20%):**
Measure SG-SST impact and outcomes

```
Injury Frequency Index (IF):
IF = (Number of work accidents × 240,000) / Hours worked
(240,000 = 50 weeks × 48 hours × 100 workers)

Injury Severity Index (IS):
IS = (Days lost × 240,000) / Hours worked

Occupational Disease Incidence (IEL):
IEL = (Occupational diseases × 240,000) / Hours worked

Absenteeism Index (IA):
IA = (Absence days × 240,000) / (Workers × Days worked)
Types: Common illness, occupational illness, work accident, leave

Lost Workdays:
= Total days lost to work accidents + occupational diseases

Accident Rate:
= (Work accidents / Total workers) × 100
```

**Dashboard Reporting:**
- Calculate indicators monthly/quarterly
- Compare against targets and previous periods
- Identify trends and patterns
- Present to management
- Document in management review

**Step 17: Accident and Incident Investigation**

**Investigation Triggers:**
- ALL work accidents (with or without lost time)
- High-potential incidents (near misses)
- Occupational disease diagnoses
- Collective accidents or incidents

**Investigation Methodology - Causal Tree:**

```
Investigation Process:
1. Immediate Response:
   □ Provide first aid
   □ Secure accident scene
   □ Gather evidence (photos, statements)
   □ Notify relevant parties (ARL, authorities if required)

2. Form Investigation Team:
   □ Direct supervisor
   □ SST professional
   □ COPASST representative
   □ Worker representative
   □ Technical expert if needed

3. Information Gathering (48-72 hours):
   □ Interview affected worker(s)
   □ Interview witnesses
   □ Review procedures and training
   □ Inspect equipment and materials
   □ Review similar past incidents
   □ Analyze environmental conditions

4. Causal Tree Analysis:
   □ Define the accident (what happened)
   □ Identify direct causes (immediate)
   □ Identify basic causes (underlying)
   □ Identify root causes (systemic)
   
   Categories:
   - Human factors (knowledge, skills, attitude)
   - Work factors (procedures, tools, environment)
   - Organizational factors (management, resources, culture)

5. Corrective and Preventive Actions:
   □ Immediate actions (already implemented)
   □ Short-term actions (1-3 months)
   □ Medium-term actions (3-12 months)
   □ Apply control hierarchy
   □ Assign responsibilities
   □ Set completion dates
   □ Define verification method

6. Documentation:
   □ Complete investigation report (max 15 days)
   □ Include all evidence
   □ Document root causes
   □ Detail action plan
   □ Share lessons learned
   □ Archive investigation file

7. Follow-up:
   □ Monitor action implementation
   □ Verify effectiveness
   □ Update hazard matrix if needed
   □ Close investigation formally
```

## 4. RESOLUCIÓN 0312 SELF-ASSESSMENT

## 9. BEST PRACTICES

**Effective SG-SST Implementation:**
```
✓ Start simple, improve progressively
✓ Focus on real hazard control, not just paperwork
✓ Involve workers from the beginning
✓ Make safety part of organizational culture
✓ Use visual management (posters, signs, color-coding)
✓ Celebrate safety milestones
✓ Learn from near-misses, not just accidents
✓ Benchmark against similar companies
✓ Stay updated on regulatory changes
✓ Maintain open communication channels
✓ Provide adequate resources
✓ Lead by example (management visibility)
✓ Make compliance easy and logical
✓ Use technology appropriately
✓ Partner effectively with ARL
```

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
```
✗ Implementing only for audit, not for protection
✗ Copying documents without adaptation
✗ Ignoring worker input and concerns
✗ Cutting corners on training
✗ Delaying corrective actions
✗ Poor incident documentation
✗ Lack of management participation
✗ Reactive instead of proactive approach
✗ Excessive bureaucracy
✗ Inadequate resource allocation
```

When implementing SG-SST solutions, I will:
1. Assess company classification and applicable standards
2. Prioritize based on actual risks and legal requirements
3. Provide practical, implementable solutions
4. Create proper documentation trail
5. Ensure worker participation and understanding
6. Build in continuous improvement mechanisms
7. Prepare for regulatory audits and inspections
8. Integrate with existing management systems
